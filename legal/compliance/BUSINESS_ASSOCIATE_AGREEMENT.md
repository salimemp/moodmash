# Business Associate Agreement (BAA)

**Effective Date:** January 17, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0

## Parties

This Business Associate Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between:

**Covered Entity:** The healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse that uses MoodMash services ("Covered Entity" or "You")

**Business Associate:** MoodMash Inc. ("Business Associate" or "We")

## Recitals

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides mood tracking and mental wellness services that may involve the creation, receipt, maintenance, or transmission of Protected Health Information ("PHI");

WHEREAS, Covered Entity and Business Associate desire to protect the privacy and security of PHI in compliance with HIPAA;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

## Article 1: Definitions

**1.1** Terms used but not defined in this Agreement shall have the same meaning as in HIPAA and its implementing regulations.

**1.2** "Protected Health Information" or "PHI" means individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium.

**1.3** "Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI" means PHI transmitted or maintained in electronic media.

**1.4** "Security Incident" means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations.

**1.5** "Breach" means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted by HIPAA.

## Article 2: Obligations of Business Associate

### 2.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures

Business Associate agrees to:

a) Use or disclose PHI only as permitted or required by this Agreement or as required by law;

b) Not use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate HIPAA if done by Covered Entity;

c) Use appropriate safeguards to prevent unauthorized use or disclosure;

d) Report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure not provided for by this Agreement;

e) Report any Security Incident or Breach within 24 hours of discovery.

### 2.2 Safeguards

Business Associate shall implement safeguards including:

| Administrative | Physical | Technical |
|---------------|----------|----------|
| Security Officer designation | Facility access controls | Access controls |
| Workforce training | Workstation security | Audit controls |
| Security policies | Device controls | Integrity controls |
| Contingency planning | | Transmission security |

### 2.3 Minimum Necessary

Business Associate agrees to limit use, disclosure, and requests of PHI to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

### 2.4 Subcontractors

Business Associate shall:

a) Ensure subcontractors agree to the same restrictions as this Agreement;

b) Obtain written assurances from subcontractors;

c) Remain liable for subcontractor compliance.

### 2.5 Access to PHI

Within 10 business days of a request, Business Associate shall:

a) Make PHI available to Covered Entity;

b) Make PHI available to individuals pursuant to HIPAA access rights.

### 2.6 Amendment

Within 10 business days, Business Associate shall incorporate amendments to PHI as directed by Covered Entity.

### 2.7 Accounting of Disclosures

Business Associate shall:

a) Document disclosures as required by HIPAA;

b) Maintain records for 6 years;

c) Provide accounting information within 30 days of request.

### 2.8 Compliance

Business Associate shall:

a) Make practices and records available to HHS;

b) Comply with Security Rule requirements for ePHI.

## Article 3: Permitted Uses and Disclosures

### 3.1 Service Performance

Business Associate may use and disclose PHI to perform services under the service agreement, including:

- Mood tracking and analysis
- Voice journal transcription and storage
- AI-powered wellness insights
- Health metrics monitoring

### 3.2 Business Associate's Operations

Business Associate may use PHI for:

- Proper management and administration
- Legal responsibilities
- Data aggregation services (de-identified)

### 3.3 Required by Law

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI as required by law.

## Article 4: Obligations of Covered Entity

### 4.1 Notice of Privacy Practices

Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any limitations in its Notice of Privacy Practices.

### 4.2 Changes in Authorization

Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any changes to permissions or restrictions on use of PHI.

### 4.3 Permissible Requests

Covered Entity shall not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner not permitted by HIPAA.

## Article 5: Breach Notification

### 5.1 Discovery

Upon discovery of a Breach, Business Associate shall:

a) Notify Covered Entity within 24 hours;

b) Provide:
   - Nature of the Breach
   - PHI involved
   - Individuals affected
   - Steps taken to mitigate
   - Corrective actions

### 5.2 Investigation

Business Associate shall:

a) Conduct investigation to determine cause;

b) Implement corrective measures;

c) Cooperate with Covered Entity's investigation.

### 5.3 Documentation

Business Associate shall document all Breaches and remediation efforts.

## Article 6: Term and Termination

### 6.1 Term

This Agreement is effective upon execution and continues until terminated.

### 6.2 Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate if the other materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 30 days.

### 6.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, Business Associate shall:

a) Return or destroy all PHI within 30 days;

b) Certify destruction in writing;

c) Retain no copies except as required by law.

### 6.4 Survival

Obligations regarding PHI shall survive termination.

## Article 7: General Provisions

### 7.1 Amendment

This Agreement may be amended only in writing signed by both parties.

### 7.2 Interpretation

Any ambiguity shall be resolved in favor of a meaning that permits compliance with HIPAA.

### 7.3 No Third-Party Beneficiaries

Nothing in this Agreement creates rights in third parties.

### 7.4 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by HIPAA and applicable federal law.
